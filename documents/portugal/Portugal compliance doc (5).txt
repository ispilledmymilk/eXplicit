5.2 CPCV — Promissory Contract
The Contrato Promessa de Compra e Venda (CPCV) is a legally binding promissory contract signed between buyer and seller before the final deed. While not required by law, the CPCV is used in the overwhelming majority of Portuguese property transactions and is strongly recommended. It locks in the agreed price, conditions, and timeline, and carries significant financial penalties for withdrawal.
* Deposit (sinal): typically 10–30% of the purchase price, paid by the buyer at CPCV signing.
* Buyer withdraws without legal grounds: deposit is forfeited to the seller.
* Seller withdraws without legal grounds: seller must pay the buyer double the deposit amount.
* Funds are held in the buyer's lawyer's client account — never in the agent's account.
* IMT (transfer tax) must be paid within approximately 30 days of CPCV signing, before the final deed.
* A Power of Attorney (procuração) may be granted to allow a lawyer to sign on behalf of a remote buyer.


5.3 Escritura — Final Deed
The Escritura Pública de Compra e Venda is the formal deed of sale, executed before a notary at a Cartório Notarial. The notary confirms all legal preconditions are met, presides over the signing, and issues the deed. Following execution, the transaction must be registered with the Portuguese Land Registry (Conservatória do Registo Predial) and notified to the AT.
* Proof of IMT payment required before signing — notary will not proceed without it.
* Proof of Stamp Duty payment required at signing.
* Remaining balance of purchase price is paid by certified bank cheque (cheque bancário).
