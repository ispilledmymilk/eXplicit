10. Privacy Law Compliance — GDPR in Portugal
Portugal, as an EU member state, is fully subject to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The national implementing law, Lei n.º 58/2019, adds several Portugal-specific provisions. Real estate marketplace platforms are classified as data controllers for user registration data and must implement robust consent, processing, and data rights mechanisms.
10.1 Governing Framework
* Primary law: GDPR — Regulation (EU) 2016/679, applicable since 25 May 2018.
* National law: Lei n.º 58/2019 of August 8, 2019 — Portuguese Data Protection Law.
* Enforcement body: CNPD (Comissão Nacional de Proteção de Dados).
* Age of consent for data processing: 13 years old in Portugal (Article 24, Law 58/2019).
* Data breach notification: notify CNPD when breach presents risk to fundamental rights of data subjects.
* Documentation requirement: ALL breaches must be documented internally even if formal CNPD notification is not required.


10.2 Key Platform Obligations
* Privacy by design: required — CNPD Guidance 2023/1 on organisational and security measures applies.
* Record of processing activities: maintain and make available to CNPD on request.
* Legal basis: a valid legal basis must exist for every processing operation (consent, contract, legitimate interest, etc.).
* Cookie consent: explicit prior consent required under Law 41/2004. Pre-ticked boxes are NOT valid.
* Marketing communications: all unsolicited commercial communications require a valid legal basis. High-risk sector per CNPD.
* Data Governance Act: Decree-Law 2/2025 (January 23, 2025) implements EU Data Governance Act (Regulation 2022/868) in Portugal.


10.3 GDPR Fine Structure
Violation Type
	Maximum Fine
	Examples
	Serious (Art. 83(5) GDPR)
	EUR 20M or 4% global annual turnover
	Processing without legal basis, data subject rights violations, international transfer violations
	Less serious (Art. 83(4) GDPR)
	EUR 10M or 2% global annual turnover
	Technical/organisational measure failures, DPO obligation failures
	National — large company (Lei 58/2019)
	EUR 5,000 to EUR 20,000 or 4% global turnover
	National derogation offences
	

10.4 Data Subject Rights
All of the following rights must be honoured on request. Platforms must build mechanisms to action each:
* Right to be informed (Articles 13–14 GDPR)
* Right of access — including provision of a copy of personal data (Article 15)
* Right to rectification of inaccurate data (Article 16)
* Right to erasure / right to be forgotten (Article 17)
* Right to restriction of processing (Article 18)
* Right to data portability (Article 20)
* Right to object to processing (Article 21)
