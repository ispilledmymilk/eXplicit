2. Regulatory Bodies
Portugal's real estate market is supervised by a set of distinct regulatory bodies, each with a specific mandate. Marketplace platforms must maintain awareness of and compliance with each body's requirements.
2.1 IMPIC — Primary Real Estate Regulator
The Instituto dos Mercados Públicos, do Imobiliário e da Construção (IMPIC) is the primary government regulator for real estate agents, agencies, and construction in Portugal. IMPIC is responsible for issuing, managing, and enforcing the AMI license (Licença AMI) — the mandatory operating license for all real estate professionals. IMPIC also supervises public procurement and the construction sector, falling under the Ministry of Infrastructure and Housing.
* Official website: impic.pt
* AMI license verification: impic.pt → 'Verificar número AMI' → enter license number
* Complaints against agents filed directly with IMPIC


2.2 CNPD — Data Protection Authority
The Comissão Nacional de Proteção de Dados (CNPD) is Portugal's independent data protection authority. It supervises and enforces the EU's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Portuguese Data Protection Law (Lei n.º 58/2019). Real estate marketplace platforms processing user data are classified as data controllers and are directly subject to CNPD oversight.
* Official website: cnpd.pt
* Governing law: GDPR (EU 2016/679) + Lei n.º 58/2019
* Serious violations: fines up to EUR 20 million or 4% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher
* Largest fine to date in Portugal: EUR 4.3 million (Statistics institute INE, December 2022)
* 2025–2026 enforcement focus: unsolicited electronic marketing communications — high-risk sector


2.3 AT — Tax Authority
The Autoridade Tributária e Aduaneira (AT) administers all property-related taxes, including the Municipal Property Transfer Tax (IMT), Annual Municipal Property Tax (IMI), Additional Property Wealth Tax (AIMI), and Stamp Duty. All IMT and Stamp Duty payments are made via the Portal das Finanças before deed signing. AT also holds the official VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário) for all properties, which serves as the taxable base for multiple calculations.
* Portal: portaldasfinancas.gov.pt


2.4 AIMA — Immigration Authority (New 2024)
The Agência para a Integração, Migrações e Asilo (AIMA) replaced the former SEF (Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras), which was dissolved. AIMA administers foreign national registration for Alojamento Local (AL) operators, who are required to report all non-Portuguese guest stays via the SIBA platform under AIMA's authority.


2.5 Turismo de Portugal
Turismo de Portugal processes AL (short-term rental) registrations via the national Balcão Único Eletrónico (BUE) portal at ePortugal.gov.pt. AL operator insurance details are publicly accessible on the Turismo de Portugal website, allowing guests and municipalities to verify coverage.
