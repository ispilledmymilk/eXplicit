7. Tax Reference — Property Transactions
Portugal imposes several taxes on property acquisition, ownership, and disposal. Understanding each tax's rate, calculation basis, and timing is essential for compliance platforms, listing tools, and any AVM or cost-calculator features.
7.1 IMT — Municipal Property Transfer Tax
The Imposto Municipal sobre Transmissões Onerosas de Imóveis (IMT) is the transaction tax paid by the buyer on all property purchases. It must be paid via the Portal das Finanças before the deed is signed. The notary will not proceed without proof of payment.

7.1c Public Guarantee for Young Buyers (Garantia Pública) — 2026
In August 2024, the Portuguese government introduced a state-backed mortgage guarantee programme for young first-time buyers. In January 2026, the government reinforced the programme with an additional EUR 275.8 million in guarantees (EUR 250M to CGD and EUR 25.8M to Banco CTT), bringing the total programme size to EUR 1.55 billion. This programme is directly relevant to marketplace platforms serving the Portuguese residential market, as it materially affects buyer affordability and the addressable market for properties priced between EUR 200,000 and EUR 450,000.

	PLATFORM SIGNIFICANCE: The combination of IMT exemption (up to €330,539) + Stamp Duty exemption + 0% deposit via Public Guarantee means a qualifying buyer under 35 can purchase a property up to €330,539 with no upfront costs except notary and legal fees. Platforms should prominently surface this eligibility information on qualifying listings.

7.3 IMI — Annual Municipal Property Tax
The Imposto Municipal sobre Imóveis (IMI) is an annual tax levied on property owners by their municipality. The rate is set by each municipality within a national range.
* Calculation basis: VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário — typically 30–50% below market value).
* Urban properties: 0.3% to 0.45% (each municipality sets rate within this band).
* Rural properties: 0.8% flat rate.
* Payment: Under EUR 100 — by 31 May. EUR 100–500 — two installments (May + November). Over EUR 500 — three installments (May + August + November).
* Liability: Assessed to the owner as of 31 December of the preceding year. If you buy in December 2025, the first bill arrives in April 2026.
* Exemption: Owners with residential VPT up to EUR 125,000 exempt if annual income is under EUR 153,300.


7.4 AIMI — Additional Property Wealth Tax
The Adicional ao IMI (AIMI) is a wealth surcharge on high-value Portuguese residential property portfolios. It applies to individuals and married couples whose total residential VPT across all Portuguese properties exceeds the threshold.

7.5 Capital Gains Tax
* Non-residents: 28% flat rate on the full capital gain.
* Residents: 50% of the gain is added to annual income and taxed at the individual's progressive IRS rate.
* Primary home exemption (residents only): Gain fully exempt if all proceeds are reinvested in a new EU/EEA primary residence within a defined window.
* Deductible costs that reduce the gain: IMT and Stamp Duty at purchase, documented renovation costs, notary fees, and real estate commission fees.


7.6 Agency Commission
* Standard rate: typically 5% to 6% of sale price plus 23% VAT.
* Paid by: the seller in the overwhelming majority of transactions.
* Buyer's agents: less common in Portugal than in the U.S., but present (e.g. Goldcrest). Fee negotiated separately.
