4. Listing Portal Landscape
In the absence of an MLS, Portugal's property market is distributed across a competitive ecosystem of listing portals. Unlike the U.S. system where IDX feeds create a standardized data layer, each Portuguese portal operates independently with its own proprietary feed formats, API specifications, and content policies.

4.2 Portal Compliance Rules
* AMI number must be displayed on all agency listings across all portals — this is a national legal requirement, not a portal policy.
* There is no national feed standard equivalent to RESO. Each portal has its own API and feed format. Platforms must build per-portal integrations.
* The same property is frequently listed by multiple agencies simultaneously on the same portal, which is legal under non-exclusive agency contracts.
* Sold prices are not displayed on any portal. Only asking prices are shown. Actual sale prices are held by the AT (tax authority).
* Imovirtual paid listings automatically cross-post to OLX due to shared OLX Group ownership.
* Private individuals may list on Idealista (free), OLX, and Custo Justo without an AMI license.
