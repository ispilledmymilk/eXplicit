8. Alojamento Local (Short-Term Rental) Compliance
Alojamento Local (AL) is Portugal's regulatory framework for short-term furnished rentals — properties rented for tourism purposes, typically for periods under 30 days. The regulatory environment underwent major reform in late 2024, significantly relaxing the restrictions introduced by the Mais Habitação program of 2023.
8.1 Legal Framework — 2026 Status
Decree-Law n.º 76/2024 came into force on November 1, 2024, with full practical effects from January 1, 2025. It reversed the most restrictive aspects of the Mais Habitação program and restored operator confidence in the AL sector.
Change Introduced by DL 76/2024
	Detail
	5-year renewal requirement abolished
	AL licences are now permanent — no expiry date
	Licence transferability restored
	AL licences survive property sales and transfers (subject to municipal containment zone exceptions)
	National freeze lifted
	National ban on new AL licences lifted as of early 2025
	Municipal discretion preserved
	Municipalities retain authority to declare containment zones and restrict new licences in saturated areas
	

8.2 AL Application Process
* Method: Prior notification (comunicação prévia / notificação prévia) submitted via the national ePortugal BUE portal (Balcão do Empreendedor).
* Standard response time: up to 60 working days (dias úteis) — approximately 3 calendar months. Prior document versions incorrectly stated this as 60 calendar days.
* Containment zone response time: up to 90 working days (dias úteis) — approximately 4.5 calendar months.
* Mandatory inspection window: 60 working days (standard) or 90 working days (containment zones) — same correction applies.
* If rejected, one review request is allowed — the applicant bears the associated costs.
* National capacity limit: maximum 9 rooms and 27 guests per AL unit.
AMI NOT REQUIRED: AL operators do not need an AMI license. AL is a property use registration, not a real estate brokerage license.
