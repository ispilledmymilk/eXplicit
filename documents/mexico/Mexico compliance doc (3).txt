3. AMPI Regional MLS Systems
In select tourist and coastal markets, AMPI chapters operate local MLS systems. These are voluntary for AMPI members, not nationally mandated. There is no RESO standard, no OData API, and no IDX/VOW framework — each local MLS operates its own database and rules. Platforms wishing to access MLS data in these markets must execute separate agreements with each regional MLS.

	Vallarta-Nayarit MLS

	Covers one of Mexico's highest-value foreign buyer markets. AMPI Los Cabos affiliated. Fideicomiso zone — all residential in restricted zone.

	Various AMPI Q. Roo chapters
	High foreign buyer volume. Restricted zone — fideicomiso required. Multiple AMPI chapters with overlapping coverage.

KEY RULE — Pre-Construction MLS Entry (Vallarta-Nayarit): Within 7 days of a ratified Purchase Agreement, the pre-construction/new construction listing must be entered into the MLS. The developer must demonstrate financial capability to complete construction. The lot owner must have executed a listing agreement with an MLS participant. If all lots under a blanket agreement will not be entered within 3 days of ratification, the agreement is submitted as an Office Exclusive.
NOM-247 PROFECO CONTRACT GATE — AMPI MLS Enforcement (Previously Missing): AMPI chapters in the Vallarta-Nayarit MLS now require agents to upload a valid PROFECO adhesion contract registration number before any listing can be entered into the MLS system. Listings submitted without a confirmed PROFECO contract number are rejected at the MLS entry stage. Platforms ingesting data from these regional MLS systems will not receive listings from non-compliant agents — building in a natural PROFECO compliance filter. This enforcement pattern is expanding to other AMPI chapters.
