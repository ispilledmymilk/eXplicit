8. Tax Reference — Property Transactions
Mexico's property tax system is primarily administered at the state and municipal level. Federal taxes apply to income (ISR) and sales tax (IVA). The key taxes for marketplace platforms to understand are the acquisition tax (ISAI), annual property tax (Predial), and capital gains tax (ISR on gains).
8.1 ISAI — Property Acquisition Tax
The Impuesto Sobre Adquisición de Inmuebles (ISAI) is a one-time tax on property transfer, payable by the buyer at closing. Rates are set by each state and municipality and vary significantly across Mexico.

8.2 Predial — Annual Municipal Property Tax
* Calculated as a percentage of the property's cadastral value (the municipal assessed value, typically 30–70% below market value).
* National rate range: approximately 0.1–0.45% of cadastral value per year — very low compared to U.S. or European standards.
* Paid annually, typically by 31 March. Monthly payment options available in some municipalities.
* Early payment discount: 5–25% available for payments made in January–February (varies by municipality).
* Predial is assessed against the owner of record as of 1 January of the tax year.
* Unpaid Predial is a lien against the property — platforms advising buyers should flag due diligence on Predial arrears.

8.4 IVA / VAT on Rental Income
* Short-term rentals (under 28 days, including Airbnb/VRBO-type): subject to 16% IVA on gross rental income.
* Long-term residential rentals: exempt from IVA.
* ISH (Impuesto Sobre Hospedaje — Lodging Tax): 5% in Quintana Roo and other tourist states. Applied to short-term rental income. Rate varies by state.
* Rental income (ISR): Mexican residents pay progressive rate on net rental profit. Non-residents: 25% flat on gross unless electing to file a Mexican return.
* Monthly ISR and IVA returns due by the 17th of the following month via SAT's online portal.
