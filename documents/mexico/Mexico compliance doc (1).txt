1. Market Structure — No National MLS
Mexico's real estate market operates without a national Multiple Listing Service or any equivalent centralized database. Unlike the United States, there is no federal IDX, VOW, or RESO-standard data feed framework. Agents, developers, and private sellers distribute listings independently across a competitive ecosystem of commercial portals. In certain high-activity tourist and coastal markets, regional AMPI chapter-affiliated MLS systems operate locally — but these are optional, not nationally mandated.
A further critical distinction: there is no government-issued real estate agent license in Mexico at the federal level. Anybody can legally offer properties for sale without certification. AMPI membership, while widely respected and the industry's best-practice standard, is voluntary.
