3. NAR VOW Policy: Virtual Office Website Compliance
The Virtual Office Website (VOW) is the primary vehicle for marketplaces providing enhanced data services to registered clients. VOW compliance is more rigorous than IDX, requiring a documented consumer-broker relationship before rich data can be accessed.
3.1 Registration and User Authentication
Every VOW must implement a registration wall that:
* Captures the consumer's full name and a valid email address.
* Sends a confirmation email and verifies the consumer's address before granting access.
* Requires the consumer to affirmatively accept a Terms of Use (TOU) restricting data to personal, non-commercial use.
* Prohibits redistribution or retransmission of any data.
* Maintains audit logs of registrations and listings viewed by each user to facilitate MLS compliance reviews.
3.2 Rich Data and Non-Disclosure State Constraints
VOW feeds provide access to historical sold data essential for valuation tools. As of 2026, twelve (12) states do not require public recording of real estate sale prices. VOW displays in these areas must strictly adhere to local MLS rules.
