8. CAN-SPAM and Email Marketing Compliance
The FTC's CAN-SPAM Act remains the federal standard for commercial email. Unlike European (GDPR) or Canadian (CASL) standards, CAN-SPAM does not require prior opt-in for commercial messages but imposes strict formatting and operational requirements.
8.1 Primary Purpose Test and Penalties
* Every automated listing alert, newsletter, or follow-up email must be evaluated against the 'Primary Purpose' test.
* If a reasonable recipient interpreting the subject line would conclude the message promotes a commercial product or service, all CAN-SPAM rules apply.
* Transactional messages (e.g., VOW registration confirmations) are largely exempt, provided they contain no false or misleading routing information.
* Each separate email in violation of CAN-SPAM is subject to civil penalties of up to $53,088 (2026 FTC-adjusted figure).
* Unsubscribe mechanisms must be clearly visible and all opt-out requests honored within ten (10) business days.
