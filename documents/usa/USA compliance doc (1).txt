1. National Framework and Regulatory Decentralization
The foundational structure of the U.S. MLS system has shifted from a rigid top-down mandate to a model of "unfettered local discretion" following a comprehensive risk assessment conducted in late 2025. Following recommendations from the MLS Policy Risk Assessment Presidential Advisory Group (PAG) in November 2025, eighteen major policy repeals and updates were approved to mitigate antitrust exposure and modernize operations for the 2026 calendar year.


1.1 MLS Count — Verified Figure
As of early 2026, RESO tracks just over 500 MLS systems in the United States. The National Association of REALTORS® (NAR) oversees approximately 500 REALTOR-affiliated MLSs. Any reference to "580" in prior versions of this document was incorrect and has been removed. Use 'just over 500' in all JSON metadata fields.
1.2 The 18 NAR MLS Policy Changes — Full Reference
On November 14–16, 2025, during NAR NXT in Houston, NAR's Executive Committee approved 18 comprehensive updates to the MLS Policy Handbook — the most extensive update in 20 years. These changes took effect January 2026. Five policy changes with direct platform impact were missing from the prior version of this document and are added here.
CRITICAL ADDITION — POLICY 7.87 REPEAL: NAR repealed Policy Statement 7.87 (Transmittal of Participants' Listings to Aggregators and Public Sites). There is NO longer any national framework governing whether an MLS must transmit listing data to third-party portals or aggregators. Syndication is now entirely a local MLS decision. Platforms cannot assume any national syndication right — every market now requires a per-MLS syndication agreement.

	Platform Impact

	REPEALED
	MLSs may now choose to accept open listings where permitted by state law. Update listing status logic accordingly.
	PS 7.89 — MLS Financial Penalty Cap ($15,000)
	REPEALED
	The national $15,000 MLS violation fine cap is removed. Local MLSs now set their own fine schedules with no ceiling.
	Section 5 — MLS Disciplinary Guidelines
	FULLY REPEALED
	Entire disciplinary framework removed from national handbook. Each MLS now governs its own discipline, fines, and enforcement.
	PS 7.71 & 7.73 — Offer Presentation Rules
	REPEALED from MLS rules
	Offer obligations now governed solely by NAR Code of Ethics and state law — not enforceable as MLS violations.
	

1.3 NAR Self-Certification Deadline — New in 2026
Unless indicated otherwise in the NAR 2026 MLS Handbook, all local MLS organizations were required to self-certify adoption of the updated Handbook policies by March 1, 2026. This deadline is not present in prior versions of this document and is critical for compliance auditing: any MLS that has not self-certified by this date is operating out of policy and presents elevated feed risk for marketplace platforms.
1.4 Participation Models — Thompson Broker Rule (FL, GA, AL)
Since 1991, Florida, Georgia, and Alabama have permitted non-REALTOR member access to the MLS via what is commonly called the "Thompson Broker" rule. In these three states, licensed brokers who are not NAR members retain the right to access MLS data and IDX feeds. Marketplace platforms operating in these states must account for Thompson Broker participants in their feed access verification logic.
