7. Privacy Law Compliance and the HPPA
7.1 Homebuyers Privacy Protection Act (HPPA) — Corrected Date
The Homebuyers Privacy Protection Act (H.R. 2808) amends the Fair Credit Reporting Act. It largely bans the sale of mortgage 'trigger leads'.
CRITICAL DATE: The HPPA effective date is March 5, 2026.Credit reporting agencies are prohibited from furnishing a consumer report in connection with a residential mortgage transaction to any third party unless that party has the consumer's documented opt-in consent or holds an existing relationship with the consumer.
* Real estate marketplaces partnering with lenders must completely overhaul lead-generation workflows to ensure all consumer information shared with a lender is preceded by a clear and documented opt-in.
7.2 CCPA, GPC, and ADMT — California
* Effective January 1, 2026, platforms must honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal as a valid opt-out request for the sale or sharing of personal information.
* ADMT regulations take effect April 1, 2027. Platforms using AI for listing ranking, AVM estimates, or consumer matching must provide a pre-use notice and allow consumers to opt out.
