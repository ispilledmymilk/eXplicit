10. Washington State SB 6091 — Pocket Listing Prohibition
Washington State Senate Bill 6091 was signed into law by Governor Bob Ferguson in mid-March 2026, following unanimous passage in the Senate (49-0 on February 10, 2026) and passage through the House. The law adds a new provision to Chapter 18.86 RCW (Washington's real estate brokerage law) prohibiting selective, exclusive, or private listing marketing for residential real estate.
EFFECTIVE DATE — CRITICAL: SB 6091 was signed in March 2026 but does NOT take immediate effect. The law goes into effect approximately three months after signing — June 10, 2026. Platforms should prepare for enforcement from that date, not from the signing date.


10.1 What the Law Prohibits
Under SB 6091, a Washington-licensed broker may NOT market the sale or lease of residential real estate to a limited or exclusive group of prospective buyers or brokers, or any combination thereof, unless the real estate is concurrently marketed to the general public and all other brokers.
* Prohibition: marketing to selective, private, or exclusive buyer networks without simultaneous public marketing
* Required: any residential listing a broker markets must be accessible to all consumers and all Washington-licensed brokers at the same time
* Enforcement: violations are subject to disciplinary action by the Washington State Department of Licensing


10.2 What the Law Does NOT Prohibit
* Private party sales: a seller may sell their property privately without listing it — the law only applies once a broker markets the property
* Not an MLS mandate: the law does NOT require MLS entry. Public marketing can be as simple as posting on the broker's own website
* Technology neutral: the law does not specify how public marketing must occur — no sign, open house, or MLS listing required, only that the listing be broadly accessible
* Health/safety exception: exclusive marketing is permitted where reasonably necessary to protect the health or safety of the owner or occupant


10.3 Platform Compliance Implications
* Washington platforms and brokers using private listing network models (e.g. Compass Private Exclusives) must redesign workflows before the effective date
* NWMLS already prohibited private listings — SB 6091 creates a uniform statewide standard superseding the patchwork of MLS-specific rules
* The law applies to all Washington-licensed brokers regardless of which MLS they belong to, closing the previous gap where non-NWMLS brokers could use private listing strategies
* Platforms should update their listing intake and compliance screening for Washington-state listings by June 2026
NATIONAL SIGNIFICANCE: Washington is the first state to codify a pocket listing prohibition into broker licensing law. Industry observers expect other states to introduce similar legislation in 2026-2027. Oregon, Colorado, and Minnesota have been cited as likely next movers.
