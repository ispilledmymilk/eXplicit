6. Regional MLS Compliance — Major Organizations
National platforms must reconcile the NAR baseline with the specific, often more aggressive, compliance regimes of large regional MLS organizations. The 2026 'local discretion' framework has significantly increased per-MLS variability.
6.1 CRMLS — California Regional MLS (Largest U.S. MLS)
CRMLS covers the bulk of California's residential real estate market and is the largest MLS in the United States.
* CRMLS does NOT support the DMEL category. Any listing publicly marketed must be entered within one business day and is immediately eligible for IDX and VOW distribution.
* Strict no-commingling rule: MLS-sourced data and non-MLS data must be displayed in separate sections or clearly labeled per listing.
6.2 Bright MLS — Mid-Atlantic (DC, MD, VA, PA, NJ, DE, WV)
* Bright has removed previous 'Coming Soon' status time limits, giving sellers more control over showing availability dates.
* Requires separate signed agreements for IDX data and VOW data access.
* Conducts automated audits of website displays to verify broker attribution (firm name + phone/email) on every property detail page.
* Co-mingling restrictions apply; verify current Bright MLS IDX agreement for 2026 status.


6.3 Stellar MLS — Florida and Puerto Rico
* Implemented the DMEL category on September 16, 2025.
* Agents must upload a signed Owners Exclusion Form within 24 hours of an MLS request to verify the delay period.
* Strong 'photo integrity' enforcement: severe fines for any platform that crops watermarks or uses listing photos without the listing firm's explicit license.


6.4 REBNY / OneKey MLS — New York City and Metro New York
REBNY (Real Estate Board of New York) is NOT NAR-affiliated and operates under its own distinct framework. OneKey MLS serves the broader downstate New York region.
* REBNY has its own IDX rules separate from NAR Policy 7.58.
* Exclusive listings and office exclusives play a larger role in NYC than in any other U.S. market.
* Platforms operating in NYC must execute REBNY-specific data license agreements, not standard NAR IDX agreements.
* OneKey MLS serves Long Island, Hudson Valley, and surrounding counties; standard NAR IDX rules apply to OneKey.
6.5 NWMLS — Northwest MLS (Washington State)
NWMLS is NOT NAR-affiliated and operates under its own independently governed rules.
* No mandatory buyer compensation sharing was ever required by NWMLS — the Sitzer/Burnett settlement had limited direct impact on NWMLS operations.
* IDX and data sharing are governed entirely by NWMLS rules, not NAR Policy 7.58.
* Platforms must execute NWMLS-specific participant agreements.
6.6 REcolorado — Denver Metro and Colorado
* REcolorado implemented DMEL in 2025; verify current delay period allowance.
* Standard NAR IDX rules apply; RESO Web API fully deployed.
* CCPA-equivalent Colorado Privacy Act (CPA) applies; honor universal opt-out signal.


6.7 ARMLS — Arizona Regional MLS (Phoenix Metro)
* Standard NAR IDX rules apply.
* Arizona was one of five state AGs that co-filed the FTC complaint against Zillow/Redfin (filed Oct 1, 2025).
* ARMLS is a high-volume market; platforms should verify DMEL implementation status.


6.8 ABOR — Austin Board of REALTORS (Texas)
* Texas is a full non-disclosure state. All AVM features on ABOR-sourced data must use VOW-sourced historical records only.
* Public price data does not exist in Texas; do not surface sold prices on IDX displays.
* AVMs must be labeled as estimates at all times.


6.9 Canopy MLS — Carolinas (NC / SC)
* Canopy MLS is among the largest MLSs in the Southeast.
* Co-mingling restrictions: verify current 2026 status in Canopy MLS IDX agreement.
* North Carolina and South Carolina are disclosure states; full sold price data available via VOW.


6.10 Miami REALTORS MLS — Southeast Florida
* Miami REALTORS has specific rules on the display of international listings and buyer concessions that reflect the unique demographics of the South Florida market.
* Florida is a Thompson Broker state (see Section 1.3); non-NAR licensed brokers retain MLS access rights.
* Verify current 2026 co-mingling and DMEL status with Miami REALTORS compliance team.
